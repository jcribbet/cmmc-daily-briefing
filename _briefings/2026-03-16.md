### 2. A Quiet but Major Shift in DFARS Cybersecurity Requirements Took Effect Feb 1
**Source:** Peerless | March 4, 2026
[View Article](https://www.getpeerless.com/blog/a-quiet-but-major-shift-in-dfars-cybersecurity-requirements-took-effect)

DFARS 252.204-7019 has been formally deleted and 252.204-7020 renumbered to 252.240-7997 under the Revolutionary FAR Overhaul (RFO) Phase 1 class deviations effective February 1, 2026. Legacy self-assessment pathways are now consolidated entirely into the CMMC framework under DFARS 252.204-7021. No new controls introduced, but contractors need to understand the restructured clause references in new solicitations.

### 4. Forbes: Quiet GSA Policy Shift Redefined Federal Cybersecurity Landscape
**Source:** Forbes | February 7, 2026
[View Article](https://www.forbes.com/sites/emilsayegh/2026/02/07/a-quiet-policy-shift-just-redefined-entire-federal-cybersecurity-landscape/)

GSA revised its IT security procedural guidelines in late January 2026, closely aligning civilian contractor requirements with CMMC. New standards include MFA, encryption, independent security assessments, and ongoing risk monitoring — all anchored to NIST 800-171. This effectively extends CMMC-like requirements beyond DoD to the civilian federal marketplace.

### 7. Before You Rebuild for NIST 800-171 Rev 3: Why Rev 2 Still Governs CMMC
**Source:** Alluvionic | March 13, 2026
[View Article](https://alluvionic.com/cmmc-nist-800-171-rev-2-vs-rev-3/)

Timely reminder that CMMC Level 2 is still aligned to NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2, despite Rev 3 being published in May 2024. Moving to Rev 3 prematurely creates scope confusion, extra cost, and documentation mismatch. The transition to Rev 3 is not expected before 2027 at the earliest, with full adoption likely in 2028.
